# SOP - Standard Ticket Triage

## Purpose

Provide a consistent first-response workflow for all Pine Ridge Township tickets.

## Required First Actions

1. Confirm the client: Borough Building or Police Department.
2. Confirm the user name and assigned device.
3. Confirm whether the issue affects one user, multiple users, one department, or the entire site.
4. Identify the application, printer, share, or service involved.
5. Record the exact error message when available.
6. Determine whether the issue is:
   - access/authentication,
   - hardware/performance,
   - application,
   - network,
   - printing,
   - security,
   - public-safety critical.
7. Check the relevant user-device record before recommending hardware replacement.
8. Separate confirmed facts from likely causes.

## Priority Guide

| Priority | Definition | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Critical | Public-safety or site-wide outage with no acceptable workaround | Police dispatch/CAD unavailable |
| High | Major department function blocked or active security concern | Police RMS unavailable to multiple users; suspected account compromise |
| Medium | One user's important work is blocked with limited workaround | Finance application unavailable to one clerk |
| Low | Minor issue, cosmetic issue, or reasonable workaround exists | Default printer selection incorrect |

## Required Output

Every model-assisted triage response should contain:

- Confirmed facts
- Missing information
- Business impact
- Likely causes
- Safe first steps
- What not to do
- Escalation criteria
- Confidence level
- Internal ticket note
- Client-facing update

## Do Not Do

- Do not invent credentials, IP addresses, permissions, or server names.
- Do not assume that shared township ownership means shared data access.
- Do not reset passwords before confirming identity and impact.
- Do not disable security software as a routine first step.
- Do not change police evidence, dispatch, or records data.
- Do not promise a resolution time without evidence.
